The Full Story

A Journey Built on Nigerian Grit

Erisco Foods didn't happen overnight. It was built year by year, decision by decision — always with the same north star: produce the best food products Nigeria has ever made, and make them affordable enough for every Nigerian family. Here's how we got here.

1981

The Beginning — One Plant, One Dream

Erisco Foods Limited is incorporated at Sango-Otta, Ogun State. Our founding facility begins production of tomato paste — at the time, a product dominated by expensive imports. The mission from day one: make Nigeria self-sufficient in food, starting with the one ingredient in every pot.

Late 1980s

Scaling the Tomato Empire

Erisco expands production capacity and establishes relationships with tomato farmers across the region. The Erisco brand becomes a household name in Lagos and Ogun State. Distribution begins spreading to other states as demand outpaces early capacity.

1990s

Going National — The Erisco Name Reaches Every State

A decade of sustained growth takes Erisco tomato paste from a regional brand to a national staple. We invest in logistics and distribution infrastructure, ensuring our products reach markets in every corner of Nigeria. Production capacity grows significantly to meet national demand.

Early 2000s

Diversification — Beyond Tomato

Recognising that Nigerian families need more than tomato paste, Erisco diversifies. We launch the Nagiko dairy brand and the Ric-Giko seasoning line, extending our reach into three new product categories. One company, three brands — each purpose-built for its audience.

2000s–2010s

The Farmland Investment — 2,500 Hectares

Erisco makes a defining strategic decision: instead of buying raw materials on the open market, we will grow our own. We acquire and develop 2,500 hectares of farmland, creating one of Nigeria's largest integrated food production operations. This investment locks in quality control and insulates us from supply chain volatility.

2010s

Certification & International Standards

Erisco achieves NAFDAC certification and SON approval across its full product range — a milestone that validates our commitment to quality and opens doors to export markets. Our QA processes are upgraded to international standards. Production capacity approaches 450,000 metric tonnes annually.

2020s

Erisco Tea & The Beverage Expansion

Erisco enters the premium beverage market with the Erisco Tea range — Green Tea Classic, Ginger, Lemon, and Honey variants. We also expand our ginger drink line and our Nagiko beverage portfolio, positioning Erisco as a full-range food and drink company, not just a tomato paste producer.

Today

Africa's Largest Tomato Paste Producer

Erisco Foods stands as Africa's leading indigenous food manufacturer. 3,000+ direct employees. 450,000 MT annual capacity. 2,500 hectares of farmland. Three trusted brands. And the same founding promise: quality food, at prices every Nigerian family can afford.
